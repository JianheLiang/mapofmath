The concept of a limit gives mathematics a rigorous way to describe approximation and convergence.

In calculus, limits explain:

- how a derivative can be defined from shrinking secant intervals
- how an integral can be defined from refined partitions
- how continuity can be stated without relying on vague intuition

For learners, the limit is often the point where intuitive geometry becomes formal analysis.
