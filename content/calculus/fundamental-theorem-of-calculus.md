The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus is the hinge that makes the subject cohere.

Its two classical parts explain:

- how accumulated area defines an antiderivative
- how an antiderivative computes a definite integral

Historically, the theorem transformed scattered geometric and kinematic techniques into a unified subject.
